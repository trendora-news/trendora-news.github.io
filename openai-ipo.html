OpenAI IPO: ChatGPT Is Going Public — What It Means for You

By Yash June 10, 2026 6 min read
← Back to Trendora

The company behind ChatGPT — arguably the most disruptive tech product in a generation — just made its biggest move yet: filing confidential IPO paperwork with the US Securities and Exchange Commission. OpenAI announced on June 9, 2026 that it has submitted a draft S-1, officially starting the clock on what could be the largest tech IPO in history.

💬 OpenAI's own statement: "We recently submitted a confidential S-1. We expect it to leak so we're just announcing it. We have not decided on timing yet; it may be a while because there are things we want to do that are likely easier as a private company."

The Numbers at a Glance

OpenAI's current private valuation sits at approximately $852 billion. Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are leading the process, with a target public debut as early as September 2026. Analysts expect the IPO could push OpenAI past a $1 trillion market cap — which would make it roughly 4x the size of Alibaba's landmark 2014 listing.

What Is a Confidential IPO Filing?

A confidential filing lets companies submit their draft IPO documents to the SEC for private review — without the whole world seeing their financials right away. This is standard for big tech IPOs. The company works through regulatory comments quietly, and the actual public S-1 (with full revenue, margins, risks) only drops about 15 days before the IPO roadshow begins.

Translation: we don't know OpenAI's actual revenue or profit margins yet. That's coming — and it'll be the most scrutinized document in tech history when it does.

Why Now?

Timing is everything. The filing came just days after OpenAI's main rival, Anthropic, filed its own confidential IPO on June 1, 2026 at a $965 billion valuation. This is now a race. Both companies are burning cash at extraordinary rates to build AI infrastructure, and going public unlocks a fresh flood of capital to keep up with the arms race.

There's also a legal angle: the filing came just two days after a jury dismissed Elon Musk's long-running lawsuit against OpenAI — removing the single biggest legal cloud over the company's market debut.

The Numbers Behind the Hype

Here's the part most headlines are glossing over. According to financial analysis of OpenAI's disclosed 2025–2026 figures, the company has been losing approximately $1.22 for every dollar it earns. Revenue growth is massive and real — but so are the costs. Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley have a tough job: convincing public market investors that a $1 trillion valuation is justified for a company that isn't yet profitable at scale.

What Does This Mean for Regular People?

Right now, only venture capitalists and big funds have been able to own a piece of OpenAI. An IPO changes that. Once public, anyone with a brokerage account — including retail investors in India through international investing apps — will be able to buy OpenAI stock directly. If you've ever thought "I wish I could invest in AI," this is that moment approaching.

What to Watch Next

The public S-1 filing, expected weeks before the roadshow, will be the real event. That's when OpenAI's actual revenue, cost structure, and business risks become public for the first time. Until then, we're all operating on estimates and leaks. Set an alert — when that document drops, it will move markets.

🔥 Bottom line: OpenAI going public is a landmark moment for the AI era — like Google's 2004 IPO was for the internet age. Whether it's priced fairly is a different question entirely. Stay tuned to Trendora as this story develops.
