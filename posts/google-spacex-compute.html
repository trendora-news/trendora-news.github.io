Google Is Paying SpaceX $920 Million Per Month Just to Run Its AI

By Yash June 10, 2026 5 min read
← Back to Trendora

Let that number sink in. Nine hundred and twenty million dollars. Every. Single. Month. That's what Google is paying Elon Musk's SpaceX for access to AI compute power under a deal disclosed in a regulatory filing on June 5, 2026. Over the full 32-month contract, that's roughly $30 billion.

This isn't a vague partnership announcement. This is one of the biggest companies on Earth admitting it can't build AI infrastructure fast enough — and renting it from a rocket company to keep up.

What Is Google Actually Buying?

According to the regulatory filing, Google is getting access to roughly 110,000 Nvidia GPUs — plus CPUs, memory, and all supporting components — housed inside SpaceX's xAI data centers. The deal runs from October 2026 through June 2029, with capacity ramping up through September at a reduced rate.

Google is using this compute to power Gemini Enterprise, its AI agent platform. A Google spokesperson explained the deal was signed to handle "surging customer demand" that came in "even higher than we expected." In plain terms: Google's own data centers couldn't keep up, so they went shopping.

Wait — Isn't SpaceX a Rocket Company?

It was. SpaceX merged with Elon Musk's AI venture xAI in February 2026, in a deal valuing the combined entity at $1.25 trillion. xAI had originally built the Colossus data center in Memphis, Tennessee — one of the densest GPU clusters on the planet — to run its own Grok AI models. Now that hardware is being rented out to the highest bidder.

Google isn't alone. In May 2026, Anthropic signed a deal to rent all available compute from Colossus 1 for $1.25 billion per month. SpaceX is now essentially a compute landlord — and business is booming right before its own planned IPO at a $1.75 trillion valuation.

The Deals, Side by Side

  • Google × SpaceX: $920M/month — 110,000 GPUs — Oct 2026 to Jun 2029 — ~$30B total
  • Anthropic × SpaceX: $1.25B/month — All of Colossus 1 — Through 2029 — ~$45B total

Combined, these two deals alone are worth more than SpaceX's entire 2025 revenue from Starlink, launches, and AI combined.

Why Is AI Compute So Scarce?

The short answer: Nvidia GPUs are the oil of the AI era, and everyone wants them at once. Training and running frontier AI models requires hundreds of thousands of these chips operating simultaneously. Even tech giants with massive data center budgets — Alphabet committed over $180 billion in capital expenditure this year alone — can't build fast enough to meet demand. So they rent from whoever has the hardware ready.

What This Means for the Bigger Picture

We are watching the physical backbone of AI being built in real time — and it's costing more money than most countries' annual GDP. The companies that control GPU supply, power delivery, and data center real estate are becoming the most strategically important entities in the global economy.

For the rest of us, this compute shortage is why AI products are expensive, why on-device AI is still limited, and why your Gemini or ChatGPT sometimes lags. The infrastructure gap is real — and deals like this are what it takes to close it.

🔥 Bottom line: Google renting compute from SpaceX isn't just a weird business story — it's a signal that AI infrastructure has become so valuable and scarce that even the world's richest companies can't go it alone. Welcome to the AI landlord era.
