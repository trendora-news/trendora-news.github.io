Deepfake Warning: Fake Nirmala Sitharaman Videos Are Targeting Indian Investors

By Yash June 14, 2026 4 min read

New Deepfake Scam Alert 🚨

The PIB (Prime Minister's Office Fact Check) has issued an urgent alert about AI-generated deepfake videos impersonating Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman. These fake videos are promoting investment schemes and cryptocurrency frauds.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: If you see videos of government officials endorsing "get-rich-quick" schemes, it's likely a deepfake. Share this warning with your family and friends.

How the Scam Works

  • Fake Videos: Scammers use AI deepfake technology to create convincing videos of Nirmala Sitharaman.
  • False Endorsement: In the videos, she appears to endorse cryptocurrency or stock trading schemes.
  • Trust Exploitation: Victims believe the Finance Minister is promoting the scheme, so they invest money.
  • Money Lost: After "investing," victims never get their returns or can't withdraw their funds.

Red Flags to Watch For

How to spot a deepfake investment scam:

  • Government officials promoting private investment schemes (they never do)
  • "Guaranteed returns" of 50-200% per month (unrealistic)
  • Pressure to invest quickly ("limited time offer")
  • Links to unknown investment platforms
  • Requests for personal information or bank details upfront
  • Poor video quality, stuttering speech, or unnatural lip-sync

What to Do If You Spot This

  1. Don't Invest: Never give money or personal details to these schemes.
  2. Report It: Forward the video to PIB Fact Check on Twitter (@pibindia) or to your local cybercrime cell.
  3. Warn Others: Share this alert with family, friends, and your community.
  4. If You Already Lost Money: File a police report immediately. Contact the Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in

The Bigger Picture: AI & Deepfakes

This isn't just about one video. As AI gets better, deepfakes become more convincing. We're entering an era where you can't always trust what you see. Always verify before trusting, especially with financial decisions.

Stay Safe

  • Official government announcements come through government websites and verified handles.
  • Investment schemes from government are communicated through official channels, never random videos.
  • When in doubt, check the government's official website or call the helpline.

Share this with everyone you know. Scammers are counting on people not knowing about deepfakes. Knowledge is your best defense.
