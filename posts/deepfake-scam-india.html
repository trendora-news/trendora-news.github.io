Deepfake Scam Warning: Fake Nirmala Sitharaman Videos Are Targeting Indian Investors

By Yash June 14, 2026 4 min read
← Back to Trendora

The government's official fact-checking arm, PIB Fact Check, has issued a strong public warning about a wave of AI-generated deepfake videos circulating on social media — videos that use the face and voice of Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman to lure ordinary Indians into fraudulent investment schemes.

⚠️ Official Warning: PIB Fact Check has confirmed these videos are completely fake. The Finance Minister has never endorsed any private investment platform, trading app, or cryptocurrency scheme. If you see such a video — do not click, do not invest.

What the Scam Looks Like

These deepfake videos are disturbingly realistic. Using advanced AI tools, scammers clone the Finance Minister's face, lip movements, and voice to create videos where she appears to endorse high-return investment platforms — typically promising guaranteed returns of 20–40% monthly, or "government-backed" trading schemes.

The videos are designed to look like clipped news segments or official government announcements. They circulate primarily on WhatsApp, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts — platforms where short video content spreads virally before it can be fact-checked.

Why This Is Especially Dangerous

Using a Finance Minister's likeness is a calculated move by scammers. For many Indians — especially in tier-2 and tier-3 cities — seeing a senior government official endorse a scheme removes all skepticism. The deepfake exploits institutional trust to bypass the very instincts that would normally protect someone from fraud.

With AI tools now capable of generating convincing video in minutes, this type of scam is only going to become more common and more convincing. The technology barrier to creating a deepfake has collapsed — anyone with a laptop and the right software can do it.

How to Spot a Deepfake Video

Look for unnatural blinking — deepfakes often blink too infrequently or at odd intervals. Watch the lip-sync carefully — even advanced models have subtle mismatches between mouth movement and audio. Check the edges of the face — hairline, ears, and jaw often show blurring or flickering. And most importantly: if a video is making a financial promise, treat it as fake until proven otherwise through official government channels.

What to Do If You See One

Do not share the video — even to warn others, sharing increases its reach. Report it directly on the platform (Instagram → three dots → Report; YouTube → three dots → Report). You can also report to PIB Fact Check at pibfactcheck.gov.in or on their official Twitter/X account @PIBFactCheck. If you've already transferred money, file a cybercrime complaint immediately at cybercrime.gov.in or call the national helpline 1930.

🔥 Bottom line: AI has made scams terrifyingly convincing. The best defense is simple — the government never endorses private investment schemes in viral videos. Ever. Share this with your parents and grandparents. They're the primary targets.
