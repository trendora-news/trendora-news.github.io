Google Is Paying SpaceX $920 Million Per Month Just to Run Its AI

By Yash • June 10, 2026 • 5 min read
← Back to Trendora

Let that number sink in. Nine hundred and twenty million dollars. Every. Single. Month. That's what Google is paying Elon Musk's SpaceX for access to AI compute power — GPUs and server hardware — under a deal disclosed in a regulatory filing on June 5, 2026. Over the full 32-month contract, that's roughly $30 billion.

This isn't a partnership announcement with vague "synergies." This is one of the biggest companies on Earth admitting it can't build AI fast enough on its own — and renting the infrastructure from a competitor's rocket company to keep up.

What Is Google Actually Buying?

According to the regulatory filing, Google is getting access to roughly 110,000 Nvidia GPUs — plus CPUs, memory, and all supporting components — housed inside SpaceX's xAI data centers. The deal runs from October 2026 through June 2029, with capacity ramping up through September at a reduced rate.

Google is using this compute to power Gemini Enterprise, its AI agent platform. A Google spokesperson explained the deal was signed to handle "surging customer demand" that came in "even higher than we expected." In other words: Google's own data centers couldn't keep up, so they went shopping.

Wait — Isn't SpaceX a Rocket Company?

It was. SpaceX merged with Elon Musk's AI company xAI in February 2026 in a deal valuing the combined entity at $1.25 trillion. xAI had originally built the Colossus data center in Memphis, Tennessee — one of the densest GPU clusters on the planet — to run its own Grok AI models. Now that hardware is being rented out to the highest bidder.

Google isn't alone. In May 2026, Anthropic signed a deal to rent all available compute from Colossus 1 for $1.25 billion per month. SpaceX is now essentially a compute landlord — and business is booming.

Why Is AI Compute So Scarce?

The short answer: Nvidia GPUs are the oil of the AI era, and everyone wants them at once. Training and running frontier AI models requires hundreds of thousands of these chips running simultaneously. Even tech giants with their own massive data center budgets — Alphabet has committed over $180 billion in capital expenditure this year alone — can't build fast enough to meet demand. So they rent from whoever has the hardware ready.

This is the hidden story of the AI race. It's not just about who has the best model — it's about who controls the physical infrastructure to run those models at scale. SpaceX/xAI built the biggest cluster and is now monetising it brilliantly ahead of a planned $1.75 trillion IPO.

What This Means for the Bigger Picture

We are watching the physical backbone of AI get built in real time — and it's costing more money than most countries' GDP. The companies that control GPU supply (Nvidia), power delivery, and data center real estate are becoming the most strategically important entities in the world economy.

For the rest of us, this compute shortage is why AI products are expensive to run, why wait times exist, and why your phone's on-device AI is still limited. The infrastructure gap is real — and deals like this are what it takes to close it.

🔥 Bottom line: Google renting compute from SpaceX isn't just a weird business story. It's a signal that AI infrastructure has become so valuable and scarce that even the world's richest companies can't afford to go it alone. The AI landlord era is here.
